Research Interests

My research seeks to harness the unique properties of nanoscale materials for macroscale applications. Societal challenges including healthcare and clean energy / energy storage demand new methods for controlling and fabricating materials wtih purpose-driven material properties. Building upon a rich background in nanoparticle characterization, microfluidics, bio-integrated devices, and novel additive manufacturing techniques, I focus on controlling materials from the nano to micro to meso to macroscales for bio-integrated point of care diagnostics and flexible energy storage. Highlights of some of this work are showcased below:

Epidermal Microfluidics

Integrating microfluidic channels into a soft, flexible device platform offers a new means of interfacing with the human body to perform a wide array of quantiative characterization of human sweat. Focused on using this platform for clinical diagnostics, my work has been featured at South by Southwest (SXSW) 2017 and the Museum of Modern Art in New York as part of the exhibit "Items: Is Fashion Modern".

Printing Two-Phase Composite Materials

A key challenge in additive manufacturing is controlling the material microstructure of a print. Using acoustic fields, I developed a new method for controlling and assembling microscale particles during deposition. This technique offers the ability to tailor the microstructure of printed composite filaments consisting of anistropic particles in an epoxy matrix. This is critical for advancing the ability to print precisely controlled materials with novel material properties not present in the starting materials alone.

Nanoparticle Characterization

As nanoparticles increase in ubiquity for medical therapies or as additives in materials, accurately assessing the stability of the colloidal solutions is critical for both efficacy and safety. Using absorbance spectroscopy, I developed a method for the stability assessment of colloidal nanoparticle solutions in response to an array of different solution conditions (e.g., salt type, salt concentration, pH). This method offers a much-needed tool for the rapid analysis and study of colloidal nanoparticle solution dynamics.
