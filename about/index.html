Research Interests

With a background in metallic nanoparticles (specifically gold nanorods), a large portion of my work has focused on developing new methods for characterizing nanoparticle properties. These methods enable the rapid and accurate determination of the effects different environmental factors (pH, salt concentration, surfactant, ionic species type) have on nanoparticles in suspension as well as the characterization of size and polydispersity.

I have also focused on developing new methods of assembling microscale particles for developing materials with engineered microstructures. As a result of an extensive background in microfabrication and microfluidics, I developed an acoustically-excited microfluidic print nozzle capable of controlling the position and ordering of anisotropic particles. Using this device, I demonstrated the ability to tailor the microstructure of printed composite filaments consisting of anistropic particles in an epoxy matrix. Additionally, through the microparticle control, this method can also deposit two-phase ordered structures using a single nozzle.

My current research interest is in combining my work from both areas to develop engineered materials that harness unique nanoscale properties for macroscale applications.
